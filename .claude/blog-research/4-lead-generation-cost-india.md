## CPL by industry (India)
Real estate Meta 150–700 / Google 300–1,500 (luxury 2,000–6,000) · Education Meta 80–400 / Google 150–600 · B2B/SaaS Meta 500–1,500 / Google 800–3,000 (LinkedIn ₹3,000–5,000) · Healthcare raw 150–500 (CPQL: IVF ₹2,200, hair transplant ₹4,100) · Finance/insurance Meta 400–3,500 / Google 1,000–4,000 · E-commerce 100–500. Most expensive: finance/insurance, B2B/LinkedIn, luxury real estate. India CPLs ~40–60% below US; Tier-2/3 another 40–60% below metros.

## Monthly budget breakdown
Ad spend (largest): small ₹30k–75k, growth ₹75k–3L, high-comp ₹3L–10L+. Min viable ₹500/day (₹15k/mo); recommended ₹1,000/day (₹30k/mo). Mgmt fee ₹10k–25k flat OR 10–25% of spend. Quote ad spend and fee SEPARATELY.

## Cost vs quality (DFY's strongest section — mechanism data GLOBAL)
- Cheap/bought lists convert 2.5% vs 31.3% owned/inbound (>12x gap). [geckoboard.com](https://www.geckoboard.com/best-practice/kpi-examples/mql-to-sql-conversion-rate/) GLOBAL
- Qualified lead costs 3–5x a raw lead. MQL→SQL converts ~13% → $200 MQL = ~$1,500/SQL. GLOBAL
- Reps waste ~50% of time on bad prospecting (Gartner); bad data ≈ ₹32k/$/rep/yr.
- 42% of B2B firms cite lead quality (not quantity) as top challenge (2025).
- DFY reframe: "A ₹120 cheap B2B Facebook lead at 13% qualify rate = ~₹920 per qualified lead, before any sales time."

## Hidden costs (India ₹)
CRM Zoho ₹800–2,400/user/mo · Landing page ₹5k–35k (premium ₹50k–1L+) · Creative/video ₹25k–2.5L+ · Email tools from ~₹575/mo · Telecaller ₹17k/mo avg (executive ₹39k). Angle: "true cost of a lead is the SYSTEM, not the click."
