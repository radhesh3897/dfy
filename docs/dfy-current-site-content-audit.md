# DFY Current Site Content Audit

Source reviewed: https://www.doneforyou.in/

## Purpose

This note captures the content structure and themes from the current Done For You website so future pages, SEO briefs, and blog drafts can align with DFY's existing market context. The current new website copy is still placeholder content and should be replaced later with final positioning.

## Current Site Structure

- Home: `https://www.doneforyou.in/`
- EdTech landing page: `https://www.doneforyou.in/scale-edtech`
- Real Estate landing page: `https://www.doneforyou.in/scale-real-estate`
- EdTech blog routes: `https://www.doneforyou.in/edtech/blog/[slug]`
- Real Estate blog routes: `https://www.doneforyou.in/real-estate/blog/[slug]`

## Landing Page Themes

### Home

- Positioning: B2B lead generation agency focused on qualified leads.
- Core promise: consistent qualified leads, not just form fills.
- Funnel language: ads, conversion funnel, CRM workflows, nurturing, qualification rules.
- Conversion form asks for name, mobile, and whether the visitor is running ads.
- Uses proof and review signals heavily.

### EdTech

- Audience: coaching institutes, online courses, EdTech startups.
- Main angle: scale student acquisition and improve enrollments.
- Repeated themes:
  - Lower acquisition cost
  - Course-specific funnels
  - Student-intent targeting
  - Full-funnel tracking
  - Enrollment-focused optimization
  - Growth audits and funnel setup
- Content section label: EdTech Growth Insights.

### Real Estate

- Audience: real estate developers and property businesses.
- Main angle: convert paid ad clicks into verified leads, site visits, and bookings.
- Repeated themes:
  - Intent-based buyer targeting
  - Verified lead quality
  - Site visit optimization
  - WhatsApp and CRM speed-to-lead
  - Project-specific landing pages
- Content section label: Real Estate Insights.

## Content Patterns To Reuse

- Problem-first framing: "leads are coming, but quality/conversion is weak."
- Strong distinction between lead volume and qualified opportunities.
- Sales team pain is a recurring proof point.
- Funnel system language works well:
  - targeting
  - creative/message
  - landing page
  - form questions
  - CRM
  - nurture
  - sales feedback
- Blog topics should usually answer one practical funnel problem.
- DFY should sound diagnostic and direct, not hype-driven.

## SEO/AEO Notes

- The current site targets India/local business keywords and industry-specific lead generation keywords.
- The new site can keep the broader technical SEO setup, but final metadata should be rewritten once final positioning and page copy are approved.
- Current sitemap only lists a subset of blog posts, while category pages pull more published posts dynamically. For the new Next.js site, sitemap generation should include all published posts once a blog system is added.
- Current `robots.txt` allows major search and AI user agents. The new site already has broad indexing allowed and `/llms.txt`.
